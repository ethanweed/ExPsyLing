Psycholinguistics

What is psycholinguistics?

“[…] the study of the psychological processes involved in language” - Harley (2005)

What are psychological processes?

  • 1st language acquisition
  • 2nd (or more) language acquisition
  • production of language
  • perception of language
  • semantic representation
  • sytactic parsing
  • reading
  • etc…

Roots in computer science

  • “Turing machine”
  • Generative grammar
  • Processor moving from one state to another

Roots in information theory

  • probability
  • redundancy
  • liklihood of moving one state to another (e.g. in a sentence)
  • “the cat lapped up the saucer of _____”

Psycholinguistics grows with computer theory

But still loves boxes and arrows!

  • Dual-stream model of speech
  • Hickock and Poeppel
  • Widely accepted
  • Cognitive and neurocognitive model
